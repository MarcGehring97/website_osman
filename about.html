ABOUT PORTRAITS OF FAMILIES

A family is like a tapestry woven together with threads of love, affection, and shared values. It can be comprised of many different threads, such as parents and children, spouses, siblings, grandparents, aunts, uncles, and cousins, each adding their own unique texture and color to the overall design. Just as the tapestry represents a sense of belonging and provides comfort, security, and identity, a family provides a supportive network and a sense of community. Family photos serve as the stitches that hold the tapestry together, preserving the moments, events, and memories that make up our family history. These photographs offer a window into the lives and experiences of our ancestors. By creating new family photos, we add more stitches to the tapestry, building a legacy that will be passed down to future generations and reflecting our identity and history over time.


WHY WE CARE ABOUT PORTRAITS OF FAMILIES

Uncovering old family photographs is like digging up a treasure trove of forgotten memories. These albums, some of which have been lost in time and some of which have been neglected, contain the essence of a bygone era. By wiping away the dust and rediscovering these precious moments, we can relive the excitement and emotions of a different time. Our archive, which serves as a safe haven for these memories, is now home to over 1,000 precious photographs, each of which is a unique gem waiting to be rediscovered. In a sense, we keep tradition alive by capturing new family memories with the same care and reverence as the old ones. Just as the past is preserved in the photographs we exhibit, the present will be remembered in the photographs we create today.
